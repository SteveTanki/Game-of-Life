Underpopulation - If a dead cell has zero to one (0 - 1) living neighbouring cells will die off. Survival - If a cell is alive and has two to three (2 - 3) living neighbouring cells, it will remain alive or stable. Overpopulation - If a living cell has more than three (4+) neighbouring cells, it will die off. Zombification - If a dead cell has three (3) living neighbours, it comes to life. Stable - In some cases, a cell may satisfy all four rules and still not change states in the next generation.
